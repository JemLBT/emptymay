CASE FILE 001 / POPULATION HEALTH ANOMALY

STATUS: UNRESOLVED
ACCESS LEVEL: INTERNAL
UPDATED WEEKLY

You work at the Department of Population Health when something unusual catches your attention. Male birth rates have fallen below 50%.

You stare at the figures for a moment, then check them again. That shouldn’t be possible.

For decades, the national birth ratio had remained stable, usually between 50.3% and 50.5% male. Small fluctuations happened all the time. Reporting delays. Regional inconsistencies. Data corrections. But not this.

A quiet unease settles over you as the numbers glow against the screen. No alarms have sounded. No one else seems to have noticed.

Not yet.

Do you keep the anomaly to yourself and investigate further alone, or report the findings to your supervisor before someone else discovers them first?

In this interactive fiction experience, every decision shapes the story. Your first choice begins here. Investigate alone or report the findings.
